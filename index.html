About Me

I am a physicist, currently working at the Institute for Complex Systems (ISC-CNR) in Rome, Italy. My background is in statistical physics, and my work focuses on understanding the emergence of collective behaviors in complex systems.

My research bridges the gap between theoretical models and real-world data. I investigate how local interactions give rise to global patterns in diverse fields, including epidemic spreading, social dynamics, and linguistic evolution.

My career has taken me through several international research environments, including MIT, the University of Oxford, the University of Manchester, Sapienza University, and INFM.

I earned my Ph.D. in Solid State Physics from the International School for Advanced Studies - SISSA (Trieste) and graduated in Physics from Sapienza University (Roma) with highest honors (110/110 cum laude), completing a thesis on spin glasses.

As a publicly funded scientist, I am committed to sharing science beyond academia, promoting scientific literacy, and contributing to public education.
